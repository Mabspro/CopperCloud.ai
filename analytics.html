The Tale of Two Zambias

Executive Summary

The Tale of Two Zambias

Zambia's economy operates as two fundamentally different countries overlaid on the same geography. This analysis reveals a stark pattern of extreme spatial concentration creating massive untapped opportunity in what we term the "neglected quadrants."

The Vertical Axis (Line of Rail) represents the industrial spine built 120 years ago for mineral extraction. It includes cities from Livingstone through Lusaka to the Copperbelt and Solwezi, accounting for 65-70% of national GDP with 61.3% average electrification.

The Neglected Quadrants—North-Eastern, South-Eastern, and South-Western regions—possess 70-90/100 agricultural potential but only 15-20/100 current development, creating opportunity gaps of 50-75 points.

Investment Thesis

The "bare and neglected" quadrants aren't empty—they're untapped primary-sector economies locked by predictable, solvable infrastructure constraints. This is mispriced optionality.

Electrification Heatmap
The Energy Apartheid

Electrification Heatmap

The Energy Apartheid

This heatmap reveals the electrical grid coverage across Zambia, exposing what we term "energy apartheid." The bright vertical stripe down the center represents the Line of Rail corridor with strong grid coverage, while the dark periphery shows regions with minimal or no grid access.

Key observations:

  • Lusaka and Copperbelt cities show 70-80% electrification rates
  • Rural areas in neglected quadrants show near-zero grid access
  • The 12.8x gap between urban (62%) and rural (4.5%) electrification
  • White markers indicate economic hubs, sized by GDP contribution

Strategic Implication

The electrification gap isn't just a development metric—it's a structural barrier preventing value-addition in agriculture, manufacturing, and services. Industrial-grade power is unavailable outside the spine, forcing raw materials to be trucked to Lusaka for processing.
