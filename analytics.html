Executive Summary

The Tale of Two Zambias

Zambia stands at a critical juncture. While the nation boasts immense hydroelectric potential and mineral wealth, a stark divide exists between its resource-rich regions and the communities that support them.

  • Economic Disparity: 70% of economic activity is concentrated in just 3 provinces.
  • Energy Crisis: The 2024 drought revealed the fragility of a hydro-dependent grid.
  • Opportunity: A $12B investment gap in decentralized energy infrastructure offers high-yield potential.

Bridging the gap through data-driven infrastructure investment.

Climate Resilience

Data Point: Kariba Dam levels dropped to record lows, reducing generation capacity by 40%.

Strategic Recommendations

Investment Framework

To unlock Zambia's potential, we propose a three-pronged strategy focused on decentralization, diversification, and digitalization.

1. Decentralize

Deploy mini-grids in "High Growth / Low Energy" zones to jumpstart local economies immediately.

2. Diversify

Invest in solar and wind to balance the hydro-heavy grid and mitigate drought risks.

3. Digitalize

Implement smart metering and AI-driven load balancing to optimize existing assets.
